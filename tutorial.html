01 · clay making

Speckled Field · hand-built bowl

Step 1 / 9

Split the clay into 2 portions, roughly 1:4. Keep both portions covered so they don’t dry out.

Step 2 / 9

Make a smooth ball from the larger portion. This will become the main body of your bowl.

Step 3 / 9

Use your finger or utensil 1 to pinch a hole in the middle of the ball and gently rotate outwards.

Step 4 / 9

Slightly change the position of your hands and continue expanding out to form a bowl shape.

Step 5 / 9

Refine the edge of the bowl with your fingers and utensil 2 once you’re happy with the overall form.

Step 6 / 9

Take the smaller portion of clay and roll out a long strip with your palm to create the base ring.

Step 7 / 9

Measure the base of your bowl, join the strip into a ring, and blend the seam. Smooth where the bowl and ring meet.

Step 8 / 9

Open the webcam and scan the bowl to check if there’s improvement needed.

Step 9 / 9

Use the sponge from the box, damp it slightly, and smooth over all surfaces and edges until your piece is even and unwrinkled.

Follow the clay steps. The checker will appear at step 8.

Align your bowl inside the shape, then tap “Scan and check”.

03 · glazing

Glazing — see your bowl in color

Step 1 — Lock in your bowl

  1. Use the scan on the right to “lock in” your bowl, then try different glaze colors to preview how they look after firing.
Bowl scan
Point your camera at the marker
Glaze colors

Step 2 — Open your set

  1. Open the Speckled Field coloring set and the brushes.

Tip: Lay down newspaper, and keep a small cup of water nearby for brush cleanup.

Step 3 — Build layers

  1. Be sure to apply multiple layers of glaze on your bowl.

Most studio glazes need 2–3 even coats. Let each coat lose its shine before the next.

Step 4 — Cross strokes

  1. Brush the stroke in opposite directions for each layer.

Example: coat 1 horizontal, coat 2 vertical, coat 3 diagonal — helps avoid streaks.

Glaze firing schedule
