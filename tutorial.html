01 · clay making

Speckled Field · hand-built bowl

Step 1 / 8

Split the clay into 2 portions, roughly 1:4. Keep both portions covered so they don’t dry out.

Step 2 / 8

Make a smooth ball from the larger portion. This will become the main body of your bowl.

Step 3 / 8

Use your finger or utensil 1 to pinch a hole in the middle of the ball and gently rotate outwards.

Step 4 / 8

Slightly change the position of your hands and continue expanding out to form a bowl shape.

Step 5 / 8

Refine the edge of the bowl with your fingers and utensil 2 once you’re happy with the overall form.

Step 6 / 8

Take the smaller portion of clay and roll out a long strip with your palm to create the base ring.

Step 7 / 8

Measure the base of your bowl, join the strip into a ring, and blend the seam. Smooth where the bowl and ring meet.

Step 8 / 8

Use the sponge from the box, damp it slightly, and smooth over all surfaces and edges until your piece is even and unwrinkled.

02 · bisque fire somewhere else

First firing · turning clay into ceramic

After your bowl is leather-hard and then fully dry, it’s ready for its first firing. This stage burns out moisture and organic materials and prepares the piece for glaze.

Place your bowl carefully in a box or padded bag and bring it to a local studio or community kiln. Tell them you’re looking for a bisque fire for earthenware clay.

03 · glazing

Coloring with Speckled Field

  1. Open the Speckled Field coloring set and the brushes.
  2. Remember: don’t add any water to the glaze.
  3. Use the coloring guide to imagine how the surface will shift after firing. Speckles will grow stronger as the glaze melts.
  4. Glaze your bisque-fired bowl evenly, inside and outside. Avoid leaving thick drips on the foot.

Let the glaze dry completely before bringing the bowl back to the studio for the final firing.

04 · glaze firing somewhere else

Second firing · melting the surface

The studio will load your glazed bowl into the kiln for a second firing. At this temperature, the glaze melts, the speckles open up, and the surface becomes food-safe and sealed.

Ask the studio which cone and atmosphere they use for this glaze. Over time, you can learn how firing conditions shape the exact color and texture of Speckled Field.

05 · receive the final piece

Bringing the bowl back home

Pick up your finished bowl from the studio and bring it back into your everyday routine — on your desk, in your kitchen, or wherever you want a quiet reminder of the making process.

Over time, Glaze Diary could store a record of each piece you fire: clay body, glaze, firing notes, and photos from daily use.
