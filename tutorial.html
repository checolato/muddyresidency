Speckled Field · beginner bowl tutorial · earthenware clay

Speckled Field · hand-built bowl

1. Clay making

  1. Split the clay into 2 portions, roughly 1:4.
  2. Make a ball from the larger portion.
  3. Use your finger or utensil 1 to pinch a hole in the middle of the ball and rotate outwards.
  4. Slightly change the position of your hands and expand out to make a bowl shape.
  5. Refine the edge of the bowl with your fingers and utensil 2 once you get the bowl shape.
  6. Take the smaller portion and roll out a long strip with your palm to make the base.
  7. Measure the base of your bowl and join the strip into a ring. Blend the ring edge with your hand and smooth the surface where they contact.
  8. Use the sponge from the box, damp it slightly, then smooth over all surfaces and edges to make sure your piece is even and unwrinkled.

When you’re ready, tap the button to run a quick check.

2. Bisque fire somewhere else

Let the bowl dry to leather-hard and then fully dry. Bring it to a local studio or community kiln for a bisque fire.

book a bisque firing session

3. Glazing

  1. Open the coloring set and the brushes. Remember: don’t add any water.
  2. Use the coloring book to visualize the color after firing.
  3. Glaze your bisque-fired bowl evenly, inside and outside.
book a glaze firing session

4. Firing somewhere else

The studio will fire your glazed bowl in their kiln. This second firing develops the Speckled Field surface.

5. Receive the final piece

Pick up your finished bowl from the studio and bring it back into your everyday routine.

Point your bowl toward your camera and tap “check my bowl”.
